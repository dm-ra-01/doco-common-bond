# Who We Serve

**Australian hospitals and the Medical Workforce teams that keep them running.**

---

## Our Target Customer

Receptor is designed for **Medical Workforce Units** — the administrative
departments within large health services responsible for:

- Planning rotation schedules for prevocational and vocational trainees
- Allocating doctors to clinical positions across facilities and departments
- Meeting Australian Medical Council (AMC) prevocational framework requirements
- Managing workforce data across multiple hospital sites

Our primary customer is the **metropolitan Australian Local Health Network
(LHN)** — a multi-site health service running a prevocational training program
of 50 or more doctors per annual rotation cycle.

---

## The Decision Maker

The decision to procure Receptor is typically made at the intersection of two
roles:

| Role                                   | Primary Concern                                     | How Receptor Addresses It                                                       |
| :------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Director of Medical Services (DMS)** | Clinical quality, AMC compliance, staff retention   | Auditable allocations; AMC framework validation; preference satisfaction scores |
| **Medical Workforce Manager**          | Process efficiency, audit trail, time-to-allocation | Eliminates manual spreadsheet work; automated matching; allocation history logs |

Secondary stakeholders include Medical Education Officers, JMO Committees, and
(crucially) the junior doctors themselves — who benefit directly from the
preferencing system.

## Active Pilot Engagement

### Monash Health

Monash Health is Common Bond's **active pilot engagement** — a major
metropolitan health service operating across five campuses (Dandenong, Casey,
Monash Medical Centre, Kingston, and Latrobe Regional) with one of the largest
prevocational training programs in Victoria.

Our engagement with Monash Health is personal as well as professional: Ryan has

workflow from the inside.

**Status:** Active pilot engagement. The FY2026 goal is a successful first full

## The Customer Profile: Why They Buy

A Medical Workforce team at a large health service purchases Receptor because:

1. **Spreadsheets are failing them.** Every year, the allocation team rebuilds a
   bespoke spreadsheet model from scratch — inconsistent, fragile, and
   unauditable.
2. **Junior doctors are asking harder questions.** Post-COVID, complaints about
   unfair allocation have risen. DMS teams need an answer that is credible, not
   just diplomatic.
3. **AMC compliance is tightening.** The 2024 AMC Prevocational Framework added
   new requirements for clinical experience breadth and audit documentation.
   Manual tracking cannot reliably meet this standard.
4. **Enterprise vendors aren't solving this.** Systems like HealthRoster are
   rostering tools, not allocation tools. The gap is real and unaddressed by the
   existing vendor landscape.
