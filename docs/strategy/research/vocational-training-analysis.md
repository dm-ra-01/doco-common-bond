# Vocational Training Market Analysis

This document provides a critical appraisal of vocational (registrar and fellowship) training specialties across Melbourne health services, identifying which programs would benefit from Receptor's preferencing and allocation service based on trainee numbers.

---

## Decision Criteria

**Threshold for Service Adoption:** ‚â•14 trainees/unaccredited registrars per specialty per allocation cycle

Programs with 14+ registrars face meaningful complexity in:
- Preference collection and analysis
- Rotation scheduling across multiple sites
- Conflict resolution and appeals management

---

## Understanding the Specialty Landscape

:::info Important Distinction
Many medical terms refer to **rotation terms** (what prevocational/vocational trainees rotate through), not separate **training programs**. Receptor can serve both:
1. **Vocational Training Programs** - Registrar allocation to health services (covered in this document)
2. **Rotation Terms** - Intern/PGY2/PGY3+ allocation to specific terms (covered in [Pricing Projections](../../operations/finance/pricing-projections.md))
:::

## Detailed Analysis by Specialty

### 1. General Practice (RACGP/ACRRM) ‚úÖ HIGH PRIORITY - MAJOR OPPORTUNITY

**The Opportunity:**
General Practice is the **single largest vocational training program** in Victoria, with 350-400+ registrars training across metropolitan and regional practices.

| **Training Region** | **2024 Registrars** | **2025 Intake** | **Notes** |
|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| **Metro Melbourne (All)** | ~200-250 | +42% growth in 2024 | Largest cohort |
| **Metro East** | ~114 (2025 intake) | Significant | General training pathway |
| **Regional Victoria** | ~100-150 | +11% rural growth | Victorian Government grants |
| **Rural Generalist** | Growing | Priority pathway | ACRRM/RACGP dual |

**Key Statistics (2024-2025):**
- **356 Victorian Government grants** offered for first-year GP registrars (2024)
- **358 new GP registrars** commenced training in Victoria (2025) - 22% increase from 2024
- **59% of grant recipients** training in regional Victoria
- **1,506 total AGPT places** nationally filled (2025)

**Critical Appraisal:**
- ‚úÖ **Massive scale:** 350+ registrars = largest single vocational cohort
- ‚úÖ **Complex rotation structure:** Registrars rotate between practices, hospitals, and community settings
- ‚úÖ **Annual matching process:** Clear preference windows
- ‚úÖ **Government incentives:** $40,000 Victorian GP training incentives driving growth
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Challenge:** Training managed by RACGP/ACRRM centrally; would need to partner with Regional Training Organisations (RTOs)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Challenge:** Many practices are private, not health service-employed

**Revenue Potential:**
- If approached as Victorian cohort: 350+ registrars = **$3,200** per match
- If approached per training region: 4 regions √ó **$3,200** = **$12,800/year**
- **Strong growth trajectory** makes this increasingly attractive

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. **Start with Existing PMCV Matches**
PMCV already coordinates several vocational matches. Demonstrating value in these could lead to broader adoption:
- RACP ATSM (Advanced Trainee Selection Matching)
- Orthopaedics Unaccredited Registrar Match
- Radiology Registrar Match
- First Year Psychiatry Training Match

### 2. **Target Emergency Medicine First**
Emergency Medicine is the "low-hanging fruit":
- Large cohorts per health service (15-30 registrars)
- Annual rotation allocation is painful
- Health services manage independently (not college-controlled)

### 3. **Basic Physician Training = Biggest Opportunity**
With 300+ trainees and complex subspecialty rotations, BPT represents the largest single revenue opportunity if positioned as a Victorian solution.

### 4. **Bundle with Prevocational**
Position vocational matching as an "add-on" to existing intern/PGY2/PGY3+ matches, offering package discounts.
